# 📚 llmbrary - Give your AI a personal library

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download-Latest_Release-blue.svg)](https://github.com/Bennyvibrionic5714/llmbrary/releases)

llmbrary turns your physical collection of books, films, and music into a digital format that AI models understand. You choose the media you own. You build a catalog. You upload this catalog into your preferred AI chat tool. The AI then references your specific intellectual history during your conversations.

## 🛠 What this software does

Most AI models lack context regarding your personal background. They make assumptions based on general data. llmbrary changes this process. It extracts titles from your photographs or text lists and converts them into a structured file. This file functions as a map of your interests. When you share this map with an AI, the model gains insight into the works that shaped your perspective.

## 📋 Requirements

You need a Windows computer to run this tool. The software requires 200 megabytes of free hard drive space. It functions best with a standard web camera or a phone to take photographs of your items. You must have an active internet connection to download the tool.

## 📷 Building your catalog

The program interface displays three main buttons: Camera, Import, and Export.

Use the Camera button if you want to scan physical items. Point your camera at a shelf or an individual item. The software recognizes covers and titles. It adds the detected media to your local database list.

Use the Import button if you keep lists on your computer. You can upload files in text, CSV, or spreadsheet formats. The system reads your titles and adds them to your library automatically.

Check the list view to verify your items. You can remove incorrect entries or add titles manually if the camera misses them.

## 📤 Exporting for AI

Once your library is complete, click the Export button. The program generates a compact text file. This file contains your metadata in a format that AI tools read with ease. Save this file to a location you can find later, such as your Documents or Desktop folder.

## 💬 Using the library with AI

Open your favorite AI chat application. Look for an attachment icon or an upload button in the chat area. Select the file you exported from llmbrary. Provide a prompt to the AI. For example: "I have uploaded a file containing my book collection. Use these books as context for our conversation today."

The AI now interprets your requests through the lens of your specific collection. It identifies connections between your books and your current projects. It understands the cultural context that informs your visual language or your approach to a problem.

## ⚙️ Troubleshooting

If the software does not open, check your system clock. Incorrect times can sometimes break security certificates. Update your Windows version if you experience performance issues.

If the camera tool fails to identify an item, confirm that the image has proper lighting. Blurry photos make it hard for the recognition system to read text. Move closer to the book spine or the cover image to improve clarity.

If the AI does not seem to recognize your file, verify that you uploaded a plain text version of your library. Do not upload encrypted or password-protected files, as the AI cannot read them.

## 🛡 Privacy and data safety

llmbrary keeps your entire collection on your local machine. No data leaves your computer until you manually upload the export file to an external AI service. You control which items remain private and which items become part of your AI context. The software does not track your habits or send usage statistics to external servers. Your catalog remains your property at all times.
